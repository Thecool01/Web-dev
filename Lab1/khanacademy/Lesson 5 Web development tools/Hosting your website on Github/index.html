Travel to Italy!

Italy can equally be called both a maritime and a mountainous country.
The territory of the country from the Alps to the Ionian Sea is more than 300 thousand sq. km.
Italy's northern neighbors are Austria and Switzerland, in the north-west it borders with France, in the north-east with Slovenia.

Holidays and weekends:

  • January 1 – New Year
  • April 25 – Day of Liberation from Fascism
  • May 1 – Labor Day
  • June 2 – Day of the Proclamation of the Italian Republic
  • August 15 – Assumption
  • November 1 – Feast of All Saints (All Saints Day)
  • December 8 – Immaculate Conception
  • December 25 - Christmas - the most solemn and beloved holiday in the country
  • December 26 – Feast of St. Stephen

Currency

Since Italy is a full member of the European Union, the country's currency is the Euro (since January 1, 2002).

Emergency numbers

  • ambulance – 118
  • police – 112
  • carabinieri – 113
  • fire department – 115
